TIDE

Framework

Geometry of non-relaxation in General Relativity.

TIDE shows that phenomena attributed to the dark sector can emerge as an effective geometric response when the spacetime connection does not relax instantaneously to Levi-Civita, giving rise to a stabilized effective torsion without introducing new fields or degrees of freedom.

Note: TIDE is not an alternative to ΛCDM, but a geometric mechanism that can explain why ΛCDM works phenomenologically in the late universe.

Validity Regime
Early universe (CMB/BBN)

In the early universe the geometry is fully relaxed (INR → 0). In this regime, TIDE recovers General Relativity exactly.

Consequence: the CMB, primordial nucleosynthesis, and early cosmology remain intact.

Cosmic voids

In low-density, low-dynamic-complexity regions, the geometric history is trivial and no non-relaxation memory accumulates.

Result: effective torsion is zero. Gravity is purely Einsteinian.

Virialized structures

In galaxies, clusters, and systems with complex dynamical history, the geometry does not relax instantaneously. The non-relaxation scalar satisfies INR > 0.

Observable effect: an effective torsion emerges and an apparent gravitational excess appears without introducing new degrees of freedom.

Geometric Mechanism
Activation rule

Effective torsion appears when the connection does not relax instantaneously to Levi-Civita, INR > 0.

In relaxed environments, INR → 0 and GR is exact.

Relaxation equation

Maxwell-type dynamics for the distortion of the connection: DΔΓ/Dτ + (1/τ)ΔΓ = S, where S represents dynamical sources (shear, tidal stress, mergers).

Qualitative prediction

The apparent gravitational excess correlates with accumulated dynamical history, not only with instantaneous mass or density.

Observational Evidence

TIDE is confronted with independent observables already available. The following tests link directly to the papers where the data, figures, and full procedures are presented.

  • Gravitational lenses: evolution of the excess gravity

    Main observable: R(z) ≡ MEinstein / M* in a homogenized compilation of 211 strong lenses (SLACS, BELLS, SL2S, HSC). A monotonic evolution with redshift is detected: 5.2σ (unbinned) and 3.1σ (conservative binned), with LOSO, bootstrap, and permutation tests.

    Paper: TIDE IV
    Sections 2–4 · Fig. 1 (p. 3), Figs. 2–3 (pp. 4–5)

    View test

  • Galaxy clusters: baryon fraction vs mass

    Independent test in N = 200 clusters. The baryon fraction fb shows a purely environmental dependence (mass / virialization), with no residual trend with redshift at fixed mass. The baryon fraction recovers the cosmological value when the effective geometric contribution is included.

    Paper: TIDE II
    Section 5 · Fig. 1 (p. 9), Table 1 (p. 10), Fig. 2 (robustness)

    View test

  • Hubble tension: environmental gradient in H0

    The discrepancy between local and early H0 emerges as an environmental geometric effect associated with light propagation through virialized regions. It is predicted and recovered that ΔH0 ≈ 2 km s−1 Mpc−1, without modifying early-universe physics.

    Paper: TIDE III
    Sections 5–6 · Figs. 4–5

    View test

  • Bullet Cluster: gas–potential offset

    In an extreme, non-relaxed system, the gravitational lensing peak remains offset from the baryonic gas. In TIDE, the offset arises as geometric memory in a strong non-relaxation regime.

    Paper: TIDE V

  • DESI: non-uniform expansion and environmental hierarchy

    Using ~50,000 DESI galaxies, a monotonic hierarchy is detected in the expansion residual ΔH from voids → sheets → filaments → knots. The effect appears in dynamical fields (ΔH, dv/dr) and not in the static potential φ, with p ≪ 10−50.

    Paper: TIDE VI

Falsifiable Predictions

The TIDE framework is not limited to reproducing existing anomalies. It produces concrete observational predictions that can be confirmed or refuted with upcoming data, without modifying standard early cosmology.

  • Saturation of excess gravity in early non-virialized galaxies

    Prediction: In young, recently assembled, and still non-virialized galaxies, the apparent gravitational excess should be reduced or absent, even at high redshift, regardless of baryonic mass.

    Falsification: The systematic detection of “mature” halos with full gravitational excess in early, unrelaxed galaxies would refute the mechanism.

    Mission: JWST (morphologies + masses), Roman Space Telescope (early lensing), Euclid (structure at z ≳ 1).

  • Lensing dependent on dynamical history, not only on potential

    Prediction: At fixed mass and redshift, the lensing effect should correlate with indicators of dynamical complexity (mergers, environmental shear, assembly), not only with the depth of the gravitational potential.

    Falsification: The absence of correlation between lensing signal and dynamical proxies, once mass is controlled, would rule out TIDE.

    Mission: Euclid (weak lensing + environments), Rubin/LSST (assembly history), DESI (dynamical context).

  • Suppression of the “dark-matter-like” effect in cosmic voids

    Prediction: In voids and low dynamical-density environments, the geometry should be fully relaxed and gravitational inference should match the observed baryonic mass.

    Falsification: Robust detection of gravitational excess in voids, not associated with prior dynamical history, would refute the model.

    Mission: DESI (environmental classification), Euclid (lensing in voids), Rubin/LSST (dynamics of isolated galaxies).

  • Environmental gradients in local expansion measurements

    Prediction: Local expansion estimates should show residuals correlated with dynamical environment (voids → filaments → knots), even at fixed redshift, without affecting the global value inferred from the CMB.

    Falsification: The disappearance of the environmental gradient with independent kinematic data would invalidate the geometric interpretation.

    Mission: DESI (kinematic reconstructions), SKA (peculiar velocities), Roman (standard distances).

These predictions delineate a clear observational regime: if upcoming surveys do not confirm dynamical and environmental dependencies, TIDE is falsified.

Einstein-Cartan Framework (ECSK)

TIDE is formulated within Einstein-Cartan-Sciama-Kibble (ECSK). In ECSK, torsion is not a propagating field and introduces no new degrees of freedom: it is an algebraic variable of the connection that switches off in the absence of effective activation. TIDE adds a single macroscopic physical hypothesis: after coarse-graining, the connection may not relax instantaneously to Levi-Civita in nonlinear processes (assembly, mergers, complex flows). This incomplete relaxation leaves a geometric memory quantified by a non-relaxation scalar INR, and GR is recovered exactly in the equilibrium limit (INR → 0).

Relaxation closure (Maxwell-type) 
D(ΔΓ)/dλ + (1/τ) ΔΓ = S

ΔΓ does not represent a new field: it is an auxiliary variable of constitutive closure (geometric delay of the connection relative to equilibrium). τ is an effective relaxation time emergent from coarse-graining; if the driving ceases, ΔΓ decays and GR is recovered.

Quasi-Newtonian limit (effective inference) 
∇·g_eff = 4πG (ρ_b + ρ_τ),    ρ_τ = ρ_b Ξ(INR)

ρ_τ is not a new physical density nor an independent source: it is an effective geometric term that modifies gravitational inference in non-relaxed environments and vanishes when Ξ → 0.

Expansion residual (linear form used in tests) 
ΔH/H0 ≈ γ ⟨INR⟩

where γ is a first-order effective coefficient (not a free cosmological parameter). This triad summarizes the mechanism: environmental effective torsion as geometric memory, standard early cosmology, and exact recovery of GR in equilibrium.

Why do we infer dark matter in observations?

The inference of “dark matter” is the result of an a priori geometric restriction: assuming that the spacetime connection is always Levi-Civita (symmetric and torsion-free). Under this premise, any curvature in excess of that produced by baryons is necessarily translated into an additional mass term in the energy–momentum tensor.

In TIDE, this discrepancy is revealed as a deficit in geometric description, not as a lack of matter:

  • The single-connection error: When reconstructing the total gravitational field (via rotation curves or lensing), it is assumed that geometry adjusts instantaneously to the present mass. TIDE introduces effective torsion as the manifestation of a geometry that has not yet returned to equilibrium after structure-formation processes.
  • Mass vs. Stabilization: What the standard model labels as “dark matter” is, in reality, the residual geometric contribution of a system undergoing stabilization. Torsion represents the energy stored in the fabric of spacetime while it seeks to recover its natural configuration.

When this geometric contribution is included in the equations, the mass required to explain the dynamics matches the observed baryonic census (stars and gas). There are no invisible particles; there is a geometry with memory that still holds the tension of the process that created it.
